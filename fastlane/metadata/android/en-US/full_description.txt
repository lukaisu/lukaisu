Lukaisu (Finnish: "a quick read-through") is an app for learning languages by reading, in the tradition of LWT — Learning with Texts.

Lukaisu is local-first: it works fully offline, with no account and no server. Add a language, paste in a text, and read it with every word colour-coded by how well you know it. Tap a word to save a translation, then practise your vocabulary with built-in spaced repetition. Everything is stored on your device.

A fresh install comes with a few starter languages and sample texts, so you can begin reading straight away.

Connecting a Lukaisu Server is optional. When you connect one, Lukaisu lights up server-powered extras: high-quality word splitting for Chinese and Japanese, text-to-speech, audio transcription, and discovering new texts to read online.

The app is a thin shell over your phone's system WebView (no Chrome dependency), so it runs on de-Googled phones. No Google account, no Google Play Services, no tracking.

FEATURES
* Read texts with tap-to-look-up words and word-status colours.
* Save words and review them with spaced repetition — fully offline.
* Create your own languages, or start from a built-in preset.
* Paste in your own texts; a few sample texts are included to start.
* Right-to-left languages such as Arabic and Hebrew work offline.
* Optional: connect a Lukaisu Server for Chinese/Japanese word splitting, text-to-speech, transcription and online text discovery.
* Works with plain-HTTP Lukaisu Server instances on your LAN.

REQUIREMENTS
None. Lukaisu works on its own — no account, no server, no internet connection required. Connecting a Lukaisu Server (free and self-hostable) is optional and unlocks the extras above.
