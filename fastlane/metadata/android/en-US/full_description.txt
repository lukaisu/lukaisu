Lukaisu (Finnish: "a quick read-through") is a mobile client for LWT — Learning
with Texts, the self-hosted web app for learning languages by reading.

Point the app at an LWT server — your own self-hosted instance, or a public one
— and read and review on your phone. It is the same pattern as the Nextcloud
mobile client: a free client for a self-hostable free server.

The app is a thin shell over your phone's system WebView (no Chrome dependency,
so it works on de-Googled phones). A small bundled connect screen asks for your
LWT server address and validates it before opening it. Your choice is remembered,
so relaunching takes you straight back in.

FEATURES
* Connect to any LWT server (3.x or newer) by URL.
* Read texts with tap-to-look-up words and status colours.
* Review your vocabulary.
* Audio playback for texts that have it.
* Works with plain-HTTP servers on your LAN.

REQUIREMENTS
You need access to an LWT server — this is a client, not a standalone app. LWT
is free and self-hostable (see the LWT project). No Google account, no Google
Play Services, no tracking.
